I.

The Whaleboat

Navis piscatoria · The New Bedford Whaling Longboat, circa 1851

⋯ ✦ ⋯

She was an old-fashioned thing; weather-stained unto darkness, her hull the hue of a November sea - black and glistening and cold; wearing upon her sides the chronicle of a hundred voyages as a veteran wears his wounds, not with shame, but with the bleak pride of one who has outlived all his enemies, and half his friends. Her bulwarks, after the peculiar fashion of that cannibal port from which she hailed, were set about with the polished teeth of sperm whales - not iron pins, as other ships employ, but ivory, yellowed and sea-burnished, so that she seemed not so much a vessel as a kind of floating jaw, grinning perpetually at the horizon. Her standing rigging was of whale-line; her blocks and dead-eyes carved from jaw-bone; her taffrail fashioned from the skull-bone of some ancient bull of the deep; and if ever a craft was built from the very substance of her prey, this was she - a thing of trophies, rigged to hunt further trophies, a cannibal of a craft, tricking herself forth in the chased bones of her enemies. She had been stove a hundred times and mended; her planks spliced and re-spliced until the timber itself seemed bewildered as to what it had originally been; and her deck - worn wan and almost white, smooth as polished ivory, by the grinding of unnumbered bare feet, feet that had long since gone to other duties or to none at all - bore upon it the indelible black marks of the try-works, of iron hooks, of lines run out so fast in the dark they smoked. She was not a ship. She was a hearse - rigged for open water, fitted for eternity, launched from Nantucket unto the ends of the earth.
