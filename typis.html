Game Design Document: Prologue

TYPIS

A Biopunk Action-Adventure · The Night The City Falls

HERO BANNER / CONCEPT ART click to set image path

Typis is a port city built on ocean biology. The infrastructure is partially alive, the power grid runs on a tidal organism, and the city is slowly dying. On the night it falls, one operative learns the cost of every form she has ever taken.

Genre Biopunk Action-Adventure
Scope Prologue · Linear
Protagonist Riven
Setting Port City · Night

01 · World

The City of Typis

CITY ESTABLISHING SHOT

The Tidal Organism

The city's power grid runs on a living creature rooted in the harbor floor. The turbines don't generate electricity: they translate the organism's pulses into usable current. When it falters, the lights do too.

Infrastructure

Skywalks of steel lattice-work arch overhead. Canals thread through markets. Sea-creature-form pumps, bent from shining metal, push currents through the city's arteries. Everything is partially alive.

Bioluminescence

Sea lanterns line the streets, casting wave-like shadows. When the lanterns die, it isn't accident. It is a message. The few who notice are the ones who have been watching all along.

The Tidewardens

City masters who maintain hydropower flow. They honor the sea and fear it in equal measure. They do not pardon those who expose what lies beneath: the vanishing ships, the twisted creatures, the price of comfort.

The Night It Falls

The turbines have hesitated before, in moments of imbalance noticed only by those watching closely. Tonight the hesitation becomes a shutdown. For the first time in a century, Typis stands still. The city that was built on constant motion, constant current, constant life. It stops.

◇

The sea is no longer waiting.

02 · Character

Riven

CHARACTER PORTRAIT: RIVEN

RIVEN

Operative · Form-Shifter · Last Assignment

Role

Operative / Bodyguard

Ability

Form-shifting via cursed ring

Status

Losing herself to the ring

Alignment

Weaponized. Unwillingly.

She twirls a grey ring around her finger, its surface engraved with worn runic letters that glimmer weakly in sparse light. To the untrained eye, just another pedestrian. Under the surface, the buzz of unhuman energy consumes her bones. With each change she is colder, further from who she really was.

The Ring's Cost

Each use of the ring costs something: identity, warmth, self. The prologue's choices around the ring are not about power. They are about what Riven is willing to lose. By the end, she is not the person who put it on.

03 · Script

Prologue Scenes

Scene 01 Surveillance at the Docks Dockyard · Canal Market · Night
SCENE 01: DOCKS

Setting

Bioluminescent lanterns. The market's din. A sea harvester approaching the pier. Riven watches from the shadows, ring on her finger, reflection writhing in the canal beneath her.

Inciting Event

The lanterns die, once, then again. The turbines hesitate. Nobody else notices. Her ring pulses. On the upper deck of the dockyard spike, a figure in dark indigo robes stands knife-edge still, holding what might be a rifle.

Player Choice: The Ring

▸Shift now, move faster, but be seen
▸Hold the shift, stay hidden, take longer
Scene 02 The Rooftop Confrontation Dockyard Spike · Upper Deck · Night
SCENE 02: ROOFTOP

Action

Riven charges. The assassin spins, steel-grey eyes wide with something like recognition. The rifle skids over the edge. They fight. Riven pins the assassin down. The ring thuds hot against their chest.

Discovery

A scar. New. Carved with the same runes as Riven's ring. She freezes.

Riven

"I don't want to kill you. Don't make me change."

Assassin

"I was dispatched to kill him. But not for what he's done. For what he's going to unleash."

Riven

"What do you mean?"

Assassin

"The sea isn't power alone. It's memory. And he's found something there. Something he won't be able to command."

Scene 03 The Shutdown Docks · Typis · Night Turning to Silence
SCENE 03: THE FALL

Climax

The deck groans. A keening whine. The turbines freeze, this time for good. The assassin breaks free, steps backward over the edge. No splash. No sound. Only fog.

Aftermath

The turbines don't start again. Typis aches, sighs, and for the first time in a century. Typis stands still. Riven turns away, the assassin's words weighing in her chest. She feels chosen. She loathes it.

Final Line

The sea was no longer waiting. And now, neither was she.
